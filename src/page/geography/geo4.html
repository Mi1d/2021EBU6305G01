Daqin was the ancient Chinese name for the Roman Empire and the Near East. With Zhang Qian's envoy to the West at the end of the 2nd century BC, the exchanges between eastern and Western civilizations gradually accelerated, and the Silk Road was officially opened. Rome was right at the end of the Silk Road. The Book of Wei Named it "Big Qin", and the Book of Later Han · Biography of the Western Regions also recorded this. Ban Chao sent his subordinate Gan Ying on a diplomatic mission to Daqin in 97 AD (the ninth year of Emperor Yongyuan of Han Dynasty). It is recorded that The Great Qin dynasty (Roman Empire) came to China five times, four of which arrived in Luoyang and one arrived in Ezhou.

Seleucia became an important trading center, a Center of Hellenistic culture, and the administrative capital of the region around 305 BC, with a population composed mainly of Macedonians, Greeks, Syrians, and Jews. At the junction of canals from the Euphrates, seleucia was the main road for land and water traffic, and between the third and second centuries the city of Seleucia rivaled Alexandria in Egypt and was larger than Antioch in Syria.

When Zhang Qian arrived in the Western regions, he reached the west of the Pamir Plateau between 129 and 128 BC, first arriving at Dayuan. Dayuan had heard that the Han dynasty was rich and hoped to establish direct contact with the Han Dynasty. When the king of Dayuan heard of Zhang Qian's purpose, he sent a guide and translator to escort Zhang Qian to Kangju. After his return, He said that Dayuan had more than 70 subordinate towns and a population of several hundred thousand. It was a prosperous country in agriculture and animal husbandry, producing rice, wheat, grapes and alfalfa, especially famous for sweating and bleeding horses.

Jade Gate Pass, commonly known as small square Plate City, was founded when Emperor Wudi of the Han Dynasty opened the road to the Western Regions and set up the four counties of Hexi. It got its name because jade was imported from the Western Regions through this road. Under the north slope of the city, there is a big east-west lane, which is the road of the central Plains and the countries of the western Regions. It was an important military pass and silk Road traffic artery in the Han Dynasty.

Chang 'an was the starting point of the Silk Road in the Western Han Dynasty. The Overland Silk Road originated in the Western Han Dynasty (202 -- 8 BC), when Emperor Wudi of the Han Dynasty sent Zhang Qian on an envoy to the Western regions to open up a land passage starting from the capital Chang 'an (today's Xi 'an), through Gansu and Xinjiang provinces, to Central and West Asia, and connecting the Mediterranean countries. The starting point of the Silk Road in the Eastern Han Dynasty was Luoyang . Its original function was to transport silk produced in ancient China, and it became a comprehensive trade road in the Ming Dynasty.

// The starting point of the Silk Road

Chang'an

Chang 'an, now Xi 'an, was the starting point of the ancient Silk Road.
The Overland Silk Road originated in the Western Han Dynasty (202 -- 8 BC), when Emperor Wudi of the Han Dynasty sent Zhang Qian on an envoy to the Western regions to open up a land passage starting from the capital Chang 'an (today's Xi 'an), through Gansu and Xinjiang provinces, to Central and West Asia, and connecting the Mediterranean countries. The starting point of the Silk Road in the Eastern Han Dynasty was Luoyang. Its original function was to transport silk produced in ancient China, and it became a comprehensive trade road in the Ming Dynasty. In 1877, the geological geologist Mr Leahy also in his book, "China" and the "from 114 BC to 127 AD, between China and central Asia, China and India with silk trade as the medium of the western region traffic" named "silk road", the term was quickly accepted by the academia and the public, and formal.
In July 2012, the UNESCO World Heritage Committee, through consultation with the governments of China and the five Central Asian countries, finally decided that China, Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan would jointly apply for this project, and the name of the application project was finally determined as "Silk Road: Road Network of chang 'an - Tianshan Corridor".

// The final site of the ancient Silk Road

Daqin

Daqin was the final site of the Ancient Silk Road. In fact, it was the ancient Chinese name for the Roman Empire and the Near East.
When our ancestors first came into contact with information about Rome, they were already convinced by their simple intuition that it was something like our divine state, and gave it a Chinese name -- "Daqin", because "its people grew up flat and resembled China, so it was called Daqin". This laudatory phrase of "having a kind of China" indeed contains a profound historical feeling echoed by the two great civilizations of China and the West.
With Zhang Qian's envoy to the West at the end of the 2nd century BC, the exchanges between eastern and Western civilizations gradually accelerated, and the Silk Road was officially opened. Rome was right at the end of the Silk Road. The Book of Wei Named it "Big Qin", and the Book of The Later Han · Biography of the Western Regions also recorded this.
Ban Chao sent his subordinate Gan Ying on a diplomatic mission to Daqin in 97 AD (the ninth year of Emperor Yongyuan of Han Dynasty). After that, there are five historical records of The Great Qin (Roman Empire) coming to China, four of which arrived in Luoyang and one arrived in Ezhou. The arrival of the Great Qin Caravan in 284 AD was the last attempt by the Roman Empire to maintain trade with China, and there is no further record of Roman envoys in Chinese historical books.
