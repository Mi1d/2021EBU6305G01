Yangzhou

Yangzhou is located at the junction of the north-south traffic of the ancient Chinese Canal and the east-west traffic of the Yangtze River. As an important port for inland navigation, Yangzhou became a famous commercial city on the ancient Grand Canal. However, Yangzhou also served as China's foreign trade port in ancient times and became a famous city on the Maritime Silk Road connecting China with the world.
Before the An shi Rebellion (755-763) of the Tang Dynasty, China's economic center was still in the Central Plains. A large number of export commodities such as porcelain and silk from the north could reach Yangzhou Port through the Grand Canal. The opening of the Grand Canal connected Yangzhou with the economically developed inland areas of the Central Plains, giving Yangzhou port a vast hinterland and rapidly rising as the richest place in the country. So in the middle of tang dynasty, there is a proverb "Yang one benifit two". Yangzhou, one of the four seaports in China recorded by Arab geographer Ibn Khurdarzbih in his famous Book Comrades in The State of Dori, was well known to Arab merchants in West Asia at that time.
As a developed foreign trade port, Yangzhou gathered a large number of people from outside the region, who either brought their religion and culture from their home country or came to China to study. Yangzhou became a center of cultural exchanges and mutual learning with other countries only second to the capital Chang 'an at that time. The most representative of the exchanges and mutual learning among civilizations along the Silk Road was the spread of religious culture.

// A city that records the cultural coordinates of land and sea in ancient China

Yazhou

Yazhou is an important ancient cultural site in Hainan. For nearly 2,000 years, Yazhou Bay has been China's southernmost trading port and an important harbor for the departure and replenishment of China's ancient maritime Silk Road. In the Han Dynasty, Generals Lu Bode and Ma Yuan, known as "Liangfubo", once traveled to and from Yazhou Bay, commanded Dingzhuya and Jiaozhi (now northern Vietnam), set up a city wall and established the boundary marker of "Tongzhu Tiannan" in Yazhou Bay.
People from the Japanese religious and cultural circles have visited Yazhou Bay for many times to pay respects to the authentic relics and trace the ruins of "Dayun Temple" to carry out cultural exchanges between China and Japan. At the end of song Dynasty and the beginning of Yuan Dynasty, Huang Daopo, China's great cotton textile reformer and ancient world-class scientist confirmed by UNESCO, landed in Yazhou Bay when he came, and left from Yazhou Bay when he returned to Japan. Huang Daopo in the bank of Yazhou bay shuinan village (now Shuinan village, big egg village area) for nearly half a century, is yazhou bay bred this outstanding women of the Chinese nation. From the Tang Dynasty to the Ming Dynasty, there were as many as 30 ministers, ministers, generals, literati and monks who were relegated and exiled to the coast of Yazhou Bay, leaving their ancient relics and historical stories.
