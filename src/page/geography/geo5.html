// The east African countries that benefited from the Silk Road

Kenya

Kenya republic is located in East Africa, the equator across the central, East Africa rift Valley north-south. It borders Somalia in the east, Tanzania in the south, Uganda in the west, Ethiopia and South Sudan in the north, and borders the Indian Ocean in the southeast, with a coastline of 536 kilometers. Lamu Islands bear witness to the ancient Maritime Silk Road and the friendly exchanges between China and East Africa 100 years ago. Today, in the region's Manda Bay, a Chinese fleet is once again "fighting day and night" in the construction of Lamu Port.
Tianji, from China Communications Tianjin Navigation Bureau, is responsible for dredging and land reclamation. As the sediment accumulates, a "new island" emerges from the water and becomes the foundation for the construction of the berth.
The lamu Port 1-3 berths construction project, contracted by China Communications, is the first project of Kenya's "Lamu Port-South Sudan-Ethiopia Transport Corridor" project, which is expected to be put into operation in 2020. The "corridor" includes plans for rail, road and oil pipelines.

// The gradual development of the European route

Russia

European parts of Russia and southern parts of Asia were connected with the Silk Road in ancient and medieval times. Its importance has come to the fore several times, especially during political instability in the Middle East and when trade links between east and West are at risk. The silk Road's several branches were the most important factor in ensuring the continuity of trade between Europe and Asia for centuries.
In modern times, the Silk Road gradually withdrew from the stage of history as the focus of international trade routes gradually shifted to the ocean. However, the cultural and economic ties established between the above-mentioned regions are still developing, affecting the development process of national culture to varying degrees. To this day, multi-ethnic and multi-religious cultures are still important factors in the social and cultural development of these regions, and the integration process of these regions has been based on mutually beneficial internal and external links in various historical periods.
Over time, as global trade moved to the sea, southern Russia retained its established network of cultural and economic ties and communications with its neighbors. Our exhibition aims to play this role by reflecting the ethnic and regional cultural characteristics of the southern regions of the Russian Federation. It was influenced by the great Silk Road that led to such union and development. The Russian state was the powerful mechanism that made the country the beginning of the east and the synthesis of different civilizations.
