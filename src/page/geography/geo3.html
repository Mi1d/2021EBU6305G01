// A link between east and West trade

Persia

The Silk Road was connected in three sections, starting with Persia and connecting China with Rome. The westernmost section begins with the ancient Greeks, or Alexander the Great, who promoted the desire and curiosity for the East; The easternmost section began in China. The Conquest of The Xiongnu by the Han Dynasty allowed the Han Dynasty to control the western regions, opening the channel of export and establishing the management and trade of merchants from the west. The middle begins with the Persian Empire, whose growth and love of commerce enabled trade between east and West.
The development of east-West trade has led to the development of cities along the Silk Road. Many small villages have turned into small towns, which have developed into big commercial cities. More and more tall buildings have been erected, connecting the eastern and western ends of the Silk Road one by one.

// An enduring eastern port on the Maritime Silk Road

Guangzhou

The History of the Guangzhou Silk Road can be traced back to the Qin and Han dynasties to the Ming and Qing Dynasties, stretching for more than 2,000 years. During the Tang Dynasty, great progress was made. The merchant ships sailed from Guangzhou southward to Tuen Mun port at the mouth of the Pearl River, then passed the seven Continents near the northeast corner of Hainan Island, passed southeast of Vietnam, passed the Strait of Singapore to Sumatra, sailed southeast to Java, left the Strait of Malacca in the west, and sailed from the West Ocean of India to the ports of Opora and Basra in the Persian Gulf.
The foreign trade of ancient Guangzhou had two characteristics: first, China's foreign trade originated from feudal society, and the object of exchange and listing was things rather than people. As the starting point of the Silk Road in Guangzhou (Panyu), early exports were mainly silk, lacquer, iron ware, while imports were hornhorn, abecas and other material trading, never slave trading, reflecting the historical tradition of civilized trade. Second, Guangzhou is the oldest and the only unfailing foreign trade port in The history of China.
